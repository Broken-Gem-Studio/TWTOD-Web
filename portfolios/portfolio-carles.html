Carles Homs - Game Designer & Gameplay Programmer

“10 milliseconds can make all the difference.” Hi, I’m Carles, and I’ve been working as a designer and gameplay programmer at Broken Gem for the past 4 months.

For the longest time I’ve had a burning passion for games as a unique medium of entertainment, both to make players the protagonists of their own stories and give them the freedom to make meaningful choices, learn from them, and formulate strategies. On the other hand, I love to code, to build intricate systems and see the gears start turning as I create a logical machine that achieves the desired goal in the most elegant and optimal way. I took this two concepts that fascinate me and decided to pursue them thoroughly to be capable of ideating fun, deep, and unique gameplay mechanics, envision them into working code, build them, and iterate them into technical and design perfection. I took these goals to heart when helping to make this project come to life, and I’m very satisfied with the results.

I mostly worked on the player characters. As a designer, I helped to make the GDD and take the initial design choices for the game, especially the character’s possible actions, combat abilities, characteristics, items, etc. As a gameplay programmer, I had to implement each of the designed features for the characters with Lua scripting and iterate them to see if they achieved the desired design results we wanted, and either fix, improve, or discard them.

Additionally, I developed the character’s score system and scene, and spent a great amount of time working as QA for the programmers, reporting engine bugs and requesting new tools and features, amounting up to 74 reports, almost half the total engine repository’s Issues.

PROJECT CONTRIBUTION

  • Character Progression Design: I made the first design and code iterations of a progression system based on stats, skill trees, consumables, and equipment. Some of these features were cut for different reasons, but others remained such as the potions and score systems.
  • Character Gameplay Design: I participated along the rest of the design team on most of the core design decisions of the game, but focused mostly on the characters in the aspects of gameplay: what can they do, how to they control, and what gives them a unique playstyle.
  • Character Gameplay Implementation: I scripted everything that the players can do: states, movements, attacks, abilities... and made implementations related to other systems such as the Lua code for animations, particles, audio, and some character-camera interactions.
  • Character Polish and QA: I tried to go the extra mile to make character gameplay intuitive, satisfying, and responsive, iterating it over and over until everything worked and felt exactly as we wanted, such as action timing, action feedback, and responsive controls.
  • Score System and Scene: I designed and crafted the level end score scene and the score system for the characters where they compete against each other in regards to their gameplay performance, which includes combat, exploration, and cooperation.
  • Broken Engine QA: I spent a significant amount of time working as an engine QA, meticulously reporting any bugs and requesting potentially useful tools and enhancements from the programmers, amounting up to 74 Issues, almost half of all reports made.
